Alexander Fischer

After trying over 40 criminal jury trials, Alexander Fischer transitioned to the civil side of law. Continuing to use his courtroom skills and experience, Alexander handles business and contract litigation for Rossman Legal. Before becoming an associate, Alexander was an Assistant State Attorney in Broward County for just under 6 years. While at the State Attorney’s Office, Alexander moved up quickly through the ranks and became a Felony Trial Unit Supervisor after just four years. While in that position, Alexander was in charge of supervising up to twelve felony attorneys and also handled cases in Miami and Palm Beach counties under Executive Assignment from the Governor. Alexander earned his Bachelor of Arts and Juris Doctorate from the University of Florida. After spending seven years in Gainesville, FL working towards his degrees, Alexander moved back down to South Florida, where he was born and raised. While Alexander’s focus is on civil litigation, he also assists Partner Gregg Rossman in the representation of police departments in Broward County. Whether it is forfeiture work, legal advising, or officer training, Alexander’s prior experience allows for an easy transition into this field. Furthermore, Alexander excelled in handling real estate law and representing associations after leaving the State Attorney’s Office. The Firm, Rossman Legal, handles Business and Contract Litigation, Contract Law and Negotiation, Probate, Trusts and Estate work, and Police Representation.
